--- layout: guide description: Home Assistant Yellow guideName: Home Assistant Yellow Power-Over-Ethernet Kit needs-install: true procedures: - title: Setting up the hardware steps: - title: Gather the required tools. preTitle: Step 1 image: step-1.png content: |
  • Home Assistant Yellow
  • 2 heat pads: heat pad memory, heat pad SOC
  • Heat sink
  • Ethernet cable
  • Raspberry Pi CM4 Module (not part of Kit)
  • NVMe M.2 SSD drive (optional, not part of kit)
  • No. 1 hex screwdriver (required for NVMe drive)
- title: Ground yourself. preTitle: Step 2 image: step-2.jpeg content: |
  • Touch something that has a direct conductive path to the earth, such as a water pipe, a wooden table, or the exterior metal box of your desktop computer.
  • This protects the components from the static electricity that is naturally present in your body - grounding is an extra safeguard.
- title: Remove the translucent enclosure, preTitle: Step 3 image: step-3.png content: |
  • using the 4 screws on the bottom of the Home Assistant Yellow Kit.
  • Keep the thumb screws close by; you will need them again later.
- title: Unpack the Raspberry Pi CM4 Module. preTitle: Step 4 image: step-4.png - title: Install the Raspberry Pi CM4 Module onto the Home Assistant Yellow carrier board. preTitle: Step 5 image: step-5.png content: |
  • Check for correct orientation of the CM4: the berry icon needs to be upside down.
  • Press the CM4 board onto the board-to-board connectors until it clicks into place.
- title: Place the heat pads onto the memory and the SOC on the CM4 Module. preTitle: Step 6 image: step-6.png - title: Install the heat sink. preTitle: Step 7 image: step-7.png content: |
  • Start by first fixating one of the plastic push pins into the dedicated hole on the carrier board.
  • Then, press the second push pin into the other hole on the carrier board.
- title: Skip to step 13 if you do not have an NVMe drive. - title: Unscrew the stand-off nut. preTitle: Step 8 image: step-8.png - title: Insert the NMVe M.2 SSD drive. preTitle: Step 9 image: step-9.png content: |
  • Notice! Do not touch the gold connector pins.
  • Hold the SSD carefully by the sides.
  • Align the notches in the SSD with the ridges in the NVMe M.2 SSD slot.
  • Then, insert at a 30-degree angle.
  • Do not force the connection.
- title: Slide the stand-off nut into the dent at the end of the SSD drive. preTitle: Step 10 image: step-10.png content: |
  • Make sure the thinner end of the nut is on top.
- title: Attach the SSD drive to the carrier board. preTitle: Step 11 image: step-12.png content: |
  • Press the stand-off nut and the SSD drive against the carrier board.
  • While pressing down the drive, turn over the carrier board.
  • From the rear side of the carrier board, insert the M2 hex screw.
  • Do not over-tighten the screw.
- title: Place the translucent enclosure ground plate on the table. preTitle: Step 12 image: step-13.png content: |
  • Make sure the extrusion is facing towards the top right corner.
- title: Place the carrier board onto the enclosure ground plate. preTitle: Step 13 image: step-14.png content: |
  • Make sure the USB connectors are facing upwards, away from you.
- title: Install the carrier board. preTitle: Step 14 image: step-15.png content: |
  • Turn the enclosure upside down.
  • Then, slide the push buttons into the openings of the housing.
- title: Insert the 4 thumb screws. preTitle: Step 15 image: step-16.png content: |
  • Do not over-tighten the screws.
- title: Insert Ethernet Cable preTitle: Step 16 image: insert-ethernet-cable.png content: |
  • Push the ethernet cable into the jack until you hear a click.
  • The other end of the Ethernet cable should be connected to your Router or Switch with POE.
- title: Get started with Home Assistant. preTitle: Step 17 image: home-assistant-mobile.png content: |
  • Initial startup may take longer depending on your internet connection.
  • Download the Home Assistant App on Android/IOS or visit http://homeassistant.local:8123 in your computer's browser.
  • Once your Yellow has initialized, the onboarding will guide you through setup on your phone or computer
  • For further information, refer to the instructions provided under Onboarding.
