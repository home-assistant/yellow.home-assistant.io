--- tags: - poe - powersupply order: 1 procedures: - title: Install the Raspberry Pi CM4 Module steps: - title: Gather the required tools. image: step-1-poe-overview.jpeg content: |
  • Home Assistant Yellow
  • 2 heat pads: heat pad memory, heat pad SOC
  • Heat sink
  • Ethernet cable
  • Raspberry Pi CM4 Module (not part of Kit)
  • NVMe M.2 SSD drive (optional, not part of kit)
  • Optional: No. 1 hex screwdriver (required for NVMe drive)
- title: Ground yourself. image: step-2.jpeg content: |
  • Touch something that has a direct conductive path to the earth, such as a water pipe, a wooden table, or the exterior metal box of your desktop computer.
  • This protects the components from the static electricity that is naturally present in your body - grounding is an extra safeguard.
- title: Remove the 4 screws on the bottom of the Home Assistant Yellow Kit image: step-3-remove-thumb-screws.jpeg content: |
  • Keep the thumb screws close by; you will need them again later.
- title: Remove the translucent enclosure image: step-4-open.webp content: |
  • Make sure the interfaces are facing towards your left.
  • Flip the case over.
  • Slightly lift off the enclosure on your left.
  • Slide the enclosure off towards your right.
- title: Unpack the Raspberry Pi CM4 Module. image: step-5-poe-unpacked.jpeg - title: Install the Raspberry Pi CM4 Module onto the Home Assistant Yellow carrier board. image: step-6-poe-install-cm4.jpeg content: |
  • The berry icon needs to be upside down: check for correct orientation of the CM4.
  • Press the CM4 board onto the board-to-board connectors until it clicks into place.
  • Make sure you press on both sides (the two long edges).
  • Verify it is parallel to the yellow board.
- title: Place the heat pads onto the memory and the SOC on the CM4 Module. image: step-7-poe-heat-pads-placement.jpeg content: |
  • Remove the protective cover from one side of the heat pad.
  • Stick the unprotected side onto the memory / SOC.
  • Remove the protective cover from the top side of the heat pad.
- title: Install the heat sink. image: step-8-install-heatsink.webp content: |
  • Push the two plastic push pins into the dedicated hole on the heat sink.
  • Start by first aligning the right plastic push pins into the dedicated hole on the carrier board.
  • Then, place the heatsink onto the CM4.
  • Lastly, take the PCB off your workplace and push the pins firmly through the carrier board.
- title: Fixate the heat sink. image: step-9-push-pins-heatsink.webp content: |
  • Take the PCB off your workplace and push the pins firmly through the carrier board.
